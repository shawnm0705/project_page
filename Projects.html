A2: Teamwork 7 days

Even if you are working on an independent project, teamwork is still a vital skill in the workplace. You may be the one responsible for the delivery of your project but you will more than likely need to engage with others to seek their knowledge, acquire data or gain valuable feedback to make your project better. In this activity you will learn some of the basic concepts of teamwork and organisational behaviour that will help you navigate the workplace.

A3: Status Reporting & Project Planning 7 days

Keeping stakeholders informed about your project and its progress is an essential skill for managing a project. It ensures that your stakeholders, sponsors and team are all on the same page and that you are continuing to meet expectations. When setting your project scope and charter you should also set up regular status meetings. This activity provides information and tasks that help you prepare for a status meeting with a project stakeholder.

A4: Communication Skills 7 days

Effective communication is an essential skill in the workplace and for managing projects and teams. With an ever increasing list of communication mediums, tools and applications as well as increasing levels of noise and distractions effective communication is becoming more complex and difficult. This activity overviews the importance of effective communication, along with some tips and resources for practical application.

A5: Shared Value 7 days

When working on projects in business or otherwise it is important to consider the social and ethical impact of decisions and actions. Shared Value is a concept coined by Michael Porter that takes social and ethical considerations of business from creating Corporate Social Responsibility projects and Foundations to give to charities to bringing social and ethical considerations into the core of business strategy.

A2: Critical Thinking 7 days

One of the key skills needed in the workplace is the ability to make decisions. Some people find decision making easy whereas others find it challenging. No matter what decision making ability one has there is always room for improvement. Critical thinking or making clear and reasoned judgement is a skill that can improve not only decision making but can be applied to help understand concepts, challenges and opportunities at a deeper level.

Innovation and Innovative thinking is an asset to any organisation attempting to be competitive in the 21st Century. In this activity you will learn basic innovation processes and how to apply them to your internship project.
